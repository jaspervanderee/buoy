    TL;DR

    Best for: Mobile-first Lightning payments with self-custody and automated channel care.

    Consider if: You expect to receive euro Lightning tips without running your own node.

    Not ideal when: You need desktop access or stronger payment privacy.

    Getting started

    1. 1. Install and secure your wallet

      Install Phoenix from the App Store or Google Play on a phone running iOS 16 or Android 8 or later. Open the app, accept the terms, and let it create the 12-word seed phrase. Write the words on paper, check the order twice, and store the copy away from your device. Enable your phoneâ€™s biometric lock so only you can open Phoenix. Never upload the seed to email, chat apps, or cloud drives.

    2. 2. Receive your first sats

      Tap Receive to generate a Lightning invoice. Share the QR code with your payer and keep the app awake until it settles, because the first euro payment may open or splice a channel. If Phoenix warns about high fees, pause and explain that the Bitcoin network is busy before retrying with a smaller amount. After success, review inbound liquidity in the Channels screen.

    3. 3. Make a payment

      Tap Send when you have a Lightning invoice to pay. Scan or paste it, read the amount and memo, then confirm. Phoenix routes the payment through its peer and shows a success banner once settled. If it hangs pending, keep the phone online because Phoenix will retry automatically. For larger euro invoices, adjust the maximum acceptable fee in Settings > Channel management before sending.

    Fees

    Scenario 1

    Paying a $15 Lightning invoice in a café

    0.4% of the amount + 4 sat

    Outgoing Lightning payments include a fixed 4 sat charge plus 0.4% routing fee.

    Scenario 2

    First $30 Lightning receive when no channel exists

    ≈1,000 sat channel open fee + mining fee

    Phoenix opens a channel for you and charges the 1,000 sat creation fee along with the current mining fee.

    Scenario 3

    Receiving a $200 payout after your channel fills up

    1% of the amount + mining fee

    Insufficient inbound liquidity triggers a splice-in with a 1% fee plus mining costs.

    Privacy & Safety

    Data flows
    • Phoenix connects to ACINQ’s server to run Lightning for you. Today, ACINQ can see who you pay and how much.
    • You can connect Phoenix to your own Electrum server to reduce what third parties can learn about your activity.
    Recovery drill
    • Your channel states are encrypted and saved with ACINQ so you can restore using your 12-word seed.
    • The seed lives only on your phone—ACINQ can’t recover it for you if you lose it.
    • If ACINQ ever disappears, go to Settings → Danger zone → Force close channels; funds return on-chain after ~720 blocks.
    • Keep Phoenix installed until the forced-closed coins show in the Final wallet view, then sweep them.
    Country caveats
    • Availability and funding options vary by country—check your app store or on-ramp.
    • Install only from the official publisher in your app store; verify the developer name.

    Migration

    Recover Phoenix after device loss or move funds to another wallet safely.

    Lost phone → Phoenix (Restore)

    Time ~10 minCost: freeRisk: low if seed is safe
    1. Install Phoenix on a new iOS 16+ or Android 8+ device.
    2. Choose Restore my wallet, enter your 12-word seed, and wait for channels to resync.
    3. Remove Phoenix from the old device once the new install works; never run both simultaneously.
    • Android transaction history will not migrate—only channel balances.
    • Keep the new device online during resync; it may take a few minutes.

    Phoenix → On-chain wallet

    Time ~1–6 hoursCost: miner fee appliesRisk: low
    1. Open Phoenix, go to Settings > Close channels, and confirm cooperative close.
    2. Wait for closing transactions to confirm (1–6 blocks); funds appear in Phoenix's Final wallet view.
    3. Sweep coins to your preferred on-chain wallet once confirmed.
    • Reserve force close for emergencies—it's slower and costs more in mining fees.
    • Check current network fees before closing; low-fee periods save sats.

    Phoenix runs a native Lightning node on the user's device, connecting to ACINQ's node for automated channel management, eliminating the need for swaps and reducing fees for direct Lightning payments. Additionally, Phoenix allows connections to a user's own Bitcoin node for enhanced privacy.

    For beginners, Phoenix's intuitive UI, low-cost Lightning transactions, and self-custodial design make it ideal for those starting with Bitcoin, though channel size limitations may require occasional fee adjustments for frequent receivers. Compared to Breez's merchant-centric features or Aqua's Liquid-focused versatility, Phoenix excels in delivering a straightforward, cost-effective Lightning experience with robust privacy options.

    FAQs

    What fees apply when I pay over Lightning?

    Phoenix charges 0.4% plus 4 sat for every Lightning payment you send.

    What happens if I lack inbound liquidity when receiving?

    Phoenix may open or splice a channel and charge 1% of the amount plus the mining fee when incoming liquidity is insufficient.

    How private are Phoenix payments today?

    ACINQ sees the destination and amount of your payments for now, although privacy upgrades are planned.

    How do on-chain deposits work in Phoenix?

    Phoenix gives you a Bitcoin address; after three confirmations the app swaps the funds to Lightning if fees are below your limit.

    How can I restore Phoenix after losing my phone?

    Install Phoenix, choose Restore my wallet, enter the 12-word seed, and let channels resync; Android history will not migrate.
