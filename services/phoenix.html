    TL;DR

    Best for: Mobile-first Lightning payments with self-custody and automated channel care.

    Consider if: You expect to receive euro Lightning tips without running your own node.

    Not ideal when: You need desktop access or stronger payment privacy.

    Getting started

    1. 1. Install and secure your wallet

      Install Phoenix from the App Store or Google Play on a phone running iOS 16 or Android 8 or later. Open the app, accept the terms, and let it create the 12-word seed phrase. Write the words on paper, check the order twice, and store the copy away from your device. Enable your phoneâ€™s biometric lock so only you can open Phoenix. Never upload the seed to email, chat apps, or cloud drives.

    2. 2. Receive your first sats

      Tap Receive to generate a Lightning invoice. Share the QR code with your payer and keep the app awake until it settles, because the first euro payment may open or splice a channel. If Phoenix warns about high fees, pause and explain that the Bitcoin network is busy before retrying with a smaller amount. After success, review inbound liquidity in the Channels screen.

    3. 3. Make a payment

      Tap Send when you have a Lightning invoice to pay. Scan or paste it, read the amount and memo, then confirm. Phoenix routes the payment through its peer and shows a success banner once settled. If it hangs pending, keep the phone online because Phoenix will retry automatically. For larger euro invoices, adjust the maximum acceptable fee in Settings > Channel management before sending.

    Scenario 1

    Paying a small Lightning invoice (e.g., $15 coffee)

    Cost today: 0.4% of the amount + 4 sat

    What's happening: Lightning routes your payment through peers; 0.4% routing fee + 4 sat flat charge.

    Keep costs low: Batch small purchases when possible; avoid many micro-payments during high network activity.

    Learn more

    Every outgoing Lightning payment includes this fee. The 4 sat is Phoenix's service charge, and the 0.4% covers routing through the Lightning network.

    Scenario 2

    First Lightning receive when you have no channel

    Cost today: ≈1,000 sat channel creation + current mining fee

    What's happening: Phoenix opens a Lightning channel so you can receive payments.

    Keep costs low: Wait for low on-chain fees (check mempool.space); receive when network is quiet.

    Learn more

    Your first Lightning receive triggers channel creation. Phoenix must commit an on-chain transaction to open the channel, which incurs the current Bitcoin mining fee plus a 1,000 sat setup charge.

    Scenario 3

    Receiving a larger payout when your channel is full

    Cost today: 1% of the amount + mining fee (splice-in)

    What's happening: Phoenix resizes your channel to accept more sats than current capacity allows.

    Keep costs low: Consolidate into fewer, larger receives; spend down balance before receiving large amounts.

    Learn more

    When incoming payment exceeds available channel capacity, Phoenix performs a splice-in operation. This on-chain transaction increases your channel size and costs 1% of the received amount plus current mining fees.

    Quick answers

    Does Phoenix charge me every time I receive?

    Not always. Once your channel exists and has room, small Lightning receives can be free aside from routing.

    When will Phoenix open or resize a channel?

    On your first Lightning receive or when more inbound capacity is needed.

    How do I avoid the 1% splice fee?

    Consolidate larger receives and avoid on-chain fee spikes.

    Key terms
    Channel open
    Creates your first Lightning channel so you can receive.
    Splice-in
    Increases channel capacity to take larger receives.
    Mining fee
    On-chain fee paid when opening/resizing; varies with network demand.

    Privacy & Safety — what Phoenix shares, how to recover, and regional notes

    Who can see what when you use Phoenix

    • Phoenix connects to ACINQ's Lightning node to route payments. Today, ACINQ can see who you pay and the amount needed to deliver your payment.
    • On-chain transactions are public by design; reuse of addresses can link activity.
    • Use your own Electrum server (Settings) to limit what third parties learn about your addresses and balances.
    • Lightning peers still observe timing/amount metadata needed for routing.
    Do: Connect your own Electrum server if privacy matters.
    Don't: Assume Lightning is fully private—some metadata still leaks via peers.

    Lost your phone? How Phoenix recovery works

    • Your 12-word seed lives only on your phone. ACINQ can't recover it for you. Channel states are encrypted and synced so they can be restored.
    • Standard restore: install Phoenix → Restore my wallet → enter seed → keep the phone online while channels resync.
    • If Phoenix/ACINQ is unavailable: Settings → Danger zone → Force close channels. After ~720 blocks, funds appear in the Final wallet; then sweep on-chain.
    Do: Store your seed offline and test a small restore when convenient.
    Don't: Run Phoenix on two phones at the same time.

    Availability & safe installs

    • App-store availability and funding options vary by country—check your local listing and on-ramp.
    • Install only from the official developer (ACINQ) on Apple App Store or Google Play.
    • Avoid third-party APKs; they're a common source of malware.

    Quick answers

    Does Phoenix work with my own node or Electrum?

    Yes. Connect your Electrum server to improve privacy; Lightning peers still see routing metadata.

    Can ACINQ recover my wallet if I lose the seed?

    No. The seed never leaves your device.

    What if ACINQ disappears?

    Use Force close; funds appear in the Final wallet after confirmations, then sweep.

    Key terms
    Electrum server
    A server your wallet queries for balances/tx history. Running your own improves privacy.
    Force close
    Shuts a Lightning channel on-chain without cooperation; slower and higher fees.
    Final wallet
    On-chain funds after channels close; sweep when confirmed.

    Migration

    Recover Phoenix after device loss or move funds to another wallet safely.

    Lost phone → Phoenix (Restore)

    Time ~10 minCost: freeRisk: low if seed is safe
    1. Install Phoenix on a new iOS 16+ or Android 8+ device.
    2. Choose Restore my wallet, enter your 12-word seed, and wait for channels to resync.
    3. Remove Phoenix from the old device once the new install works; never run both simultaneously.
    • Android transaction history will not migrate—only channel balances.
    • Keep the new device online during resync; it may take a few minutes.

    Phoenix → On-chain wallet

    Time ~1–6 hoursCost: miner fee appliesRisk: low
    1. Open Phoenix, go to Settings > Close channels, and confirm cooperative close.
    2. Wait for closing transactions to confirm (1–6 blocks); funds appear in Phoenix's Final wallet view.
    3. Sweep coins to your preferred on-chain wallet once confirmed.
    • Reserve force close for emergencies—it's slower and costs more in mining fees.
    • Check current network fees before closing; low-fee periods save sats.

    Phoenix is designed to provide a simple, self-custodial experience for fast, low-cost Lightning transactions directly on mobile devices, emphasizing ease of use for beginners.

    Phoenix is designed to provide a simple, self-custodial experience for fast, low-cost Lightning transactions directly on mobile devices, emphasizing ease of use for beginners.

    Phoenix runs a native Lightning node on the user's device, connecting to ACINQ's node for automated channel management, eliminating the need for swaps and reducing fees for direct Lightning payments. Additionally, Phoenix allows connections to a user's own Bitcoin node for enhanced privacy.

    For beginners, Phoenix's intuitive UI, low-cost Lightning transactions, and self-custodial design make it ideal for those starting with Bitcoin, though channel size limitations may require occasional fee adjustments for frequent receivers. Compared to Breez's merchant-centric features or Aqua's Liquid-focused versatility, Phoenix excels in delivering a straightforward, cost-effective Lightning experience with robust privacy options.

    FAQs

    What fees apply when I pay over Lightning?

    Phoenix charges 0.4% plus 4 sat for every Lightning payment you send.

    What happens if I lack inbound liquidity when receiving?

    Phoenix may open or splice a channel and charge 1% of the amount plus the mining fee when incoming liquidity is insufficient.

    How private are Phoenix payments today?

    ACINQ sees the destination and amount of your payments for now, although privacy upgrades are planned.

    How do on-chain deposits work in Phoenix?

    Phoenix gives you a Bitcoin address; after three confirmations the app swaps the funds to Lightning if fees are below your limit.

    How can I restore Phoenix after losing my phone?

    Install Phoenix, choose Restore my wallet, enter the 12-word seed, and let channels resync; Android history will not migrate.
