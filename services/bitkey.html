    Quick verdict

    Best for: Bitcoin savers who want Block's hardware + app bundle so they can hold two of three keys without memorizing a seed phrase.

    Consider if: You want NFC hardware, cloud backups, Recovery Contacts, inheritance tooling, and an emergency exit if Block ever goes dark.

    Not ideal when: You prefer a screen-based hardware wallet, DIY multisig, or Lightning support today.

    Set up your Bitkey vault

    Set up your Bitkey vault

    1. Choose policy & secure keys/backups

    Time: ~15 minCost: IncludedRisk: Low
    1. Pair the hardware key via NFC, enroll your fingerprint, and verify the encrypted mobile key backup saved to your cloud drive.
    2. Document who holds which key: mobile key on your phone, hardware key offline, Bitkey's server key as the third signer.
    3. Record where the hardware key and cloud backup live, and store instructions separately so one break-in cannot grab both.

    Gotcha: The hardware key has no screen; always confirm addresses on your phone or connected node.

    2. Prove the setup

    Time: ~20 minCost: Mining feeRisk: Low
    1. Generate a receive address, send a small test (~10k sats), and wait for Security hub to confirm the deposit.
    2. Simulate one failure: run Cloud Recovery on a spare phone, start hardware replacement to see the seven-day hold, or test the Emergency Exit Kit offline.
    3. Wipe the spare device after the drill and log what you learned in your vault documentation.

    Gotcha: Skipping the drill leaves recovery timing a mystery; practice now so you trust it under pressure.

    3. Operationalize safety

    Time: ~20 minCost: FreeRisk: Low
    1. Set mobile send limits so only pocket money leaves with the phone; larger amounts require the hardware key or Recovery Contacts.
    2. Enable push, SMS, and email alerts for sends, recovery attempts, and inheritance events.
    3. Invite your Inheritance beneficiary, document the six-month timer, and store key locations plus contact instructions in a safe place.

    Gotcha: Stale instructions turn convenience into a choke point; update the log when you travel or rotate devices.

    Quick answers

    Does Bitkey require me to write down a seed phrase?

    No. Bitkey replaces seed phrases with the hardware key plus an encrypted mobile key backup stored in your own iCloud or Google Drive account.

    Can Bitkey connect to my own Bitcoin node?

    Yes. Advanced settings let you point the app at your full node or Electrum server so addresses and history sync privately.

    Do I need an Android phone for the Emergency Exit Kit?

    The kit runs on Android, so keep access to a spare Android device even if your daily driver is iOS.

    Key features

    Your key policy (who holds which keys)

    Default policy: 2-of-3 multisig

    • Who holds keys: Phone (mobile) · Hardware device · Provider server
    • 1 key can: View balance / prepare only — no spending
    • 2 keys required for: Spends, limit changes, recovery, inheritance

    Good for: assisted self-custody without a seed. Trade-off: hardware key needed for big moves or outages.

    What each key does

    • Mobile + server: Daily spends up to your limit
    • Hardware + mobile: Bypass server; move any amount; change settings
    • Hardware + server: Recover when phone is lost or replaced

    Good for: quick daily use. Trade-off: keep hardware key ready for exceptions.

    If this happens, can I still move my Bitcoin?

    If this happens, can I still move my Bitcoin?

    Lose phone

    Works
    ~10 min

    An encrypted copy of your mobile key lives in your own cloud account, and your hardware key decrypts it. You can restore the wallet on a new phone without memorizing a seed phrase.

    Show steps
    1. Install Bitkey on a new phone and sign into the same iCloud or Google Drive account.
    2. Approve the cloud access prompt, then tap your hardware key to decrypt the backup.
    3. Wait for the wallet to resync and verify the balance.

    Gotcha: Cloud Recovery needs the hardware key, so keep it safe and separate from your phone.

    Lose hardware

    Works, with caveat
    7 days or instant

    Bitkey triggers a seven-day security hold when you report lost hardware, giving you time to cancel if someone else made the request. Recovery Contacts can vouch for you to skip the waiting period.

    Show steps
    1. Open the app, start hardware recovery, and watch for SMS and email alerts confirming the request.
    2. Wait seven days for the hold to clear, or share a code with your Recovery Contacts to speed it up.
    3. Pair the new hardware key and transfer your wallet to the fresh device.

    Gotcha: If you lose both phone and hardware, Recovery Contacts become your only path; invite them during setup.

    Bitkey compromised or offline

    Works
    ~20 min

    Bitkey holds only one of three keys. You control two keys, so you can always move bitcoin without the server—either by co-signing with your mobile and hardware keys, or by using the Emergency Exit Kit if their infrastructure goes offline.

    Show steps
    1. If the server is online: open the Bitkey app, generate a receive address in another wallet, and use your mobile key plus hardware key to co-sign the send.
    2. If the server is offline or compromised: download the Emergency Exit Kit from Security hub, verify the SHA256 checksum, sideload the APK onto an Android device, tap your hardware key, and sweep your balance.
    3. Confirm the transaction on-chain to complete the exit.

    Gotcha: The Emergency Exit Kit only runs on Android, so plan access to an Android device before an emergency. Different people draw different lines about how much to keep with a third-party key holder; you can choose differently.

    Lose phone and hardware

    Works, with caveat
    ~30 min + hold

    When you set up Bitkey, you can invite trusted contacts who download the app and agree to help you recover. If you lose your phone and hardware, your contacts verify a code inside their app so you can rebuild the wallet on new devices.

    Show steps
    1. Download the Bitkey app on a new phone and start the recovery process for both devices.
    2. Share a verification code with your Recovery Contacts through a separate channel.
    3. Once they approve the request in their app, recover your mobile key from the cloud and pair a new hardware key.
    4. Transfer your wallet balance to the fresh device setup.

    Gotcha: Recovery Contacts are your only path when both devices are gone; invite them during initial setup and confirm they still have access annually.

    Inheritance: pass it on safely

    Inheritance: pass it on safely

    Set this up once; your beneficiary gets a clear, documented path.

    1. Set up today

    Action
    ~20 min

    Your beneficiary must have their own Bitkey hardware wallet to accept the invitation and later claim funds.

    1. Open Security hub in Bitkey app and invite your beneficiary
    2. Wait for them to accept using their own Bitkey device
    3. Document where your hardware key and cloud backup live
    4. Store a one-page note with locations in a safe place
    5. Tell beneficiary where to find the note when needed
    Do this: Complete setup now so your beneficiary can act when the time comes.

    2. When someone passes

    Process
    6-month hold

    You'll need: Beneficiary's own Bitkey device · access to instructions you left

    1. Beneficiary opens their Bitkey app and starts inheritance claim
    2. Six-month security hold begins; you receive alerts if alive
    3. After hold clears unchallenged, encrypted App Key transfers to beneficiary
    4. Beneficiary accesses wallet and sweeps funds to their own storage

    Six-month waiting period from claim start to access. Network fees may apply for final sweep.

    Do this: Make sure your beneficiary has their own Bitkey before you need this feature.

    Privacy & Safety

    Privacy & Safety

    Who can see what when you use Bitkey

    • Bitkey keeps your email, shipping address, and notification preferences to deliver hardware and security alerts.
    • Encrypted mobile key backups live in your own cloud account; Bitkey cannot read them without your hardware key.
    • Recovery Contacts and inheritance requests are stored with timestamps so you can review who has access.
    Do: Label every device inside Security hub and enable all alert channels so you notice suspicious activity.
    Don't: Do not store your Emergency Exit instructions in a shared cloud folder that anyone can read.

    Lost your phone? How Bitkey recovery works

    • Cloud Recovery: install the app on a new phone, sign into your cloud account, and tap the hardware key to decrypt the backup.
    • Hardware recovery: start the process in the app, wait out the seven-day security hold (or have Recovery Contacts approve sooner), then pair the replacement device.
    • Lost phone + hardware: contact your Recovery Contacts, have them verify the code, and rebuild the wallet on brand-new hardware.
    Do: Keep your hardware key charged and your Recovery Contacts updated so recovery paths stay available.
    Don't: Ignore SMS or email notifications about recovery events; respond quickly so attackers cannot complete the hold.

    Availability & safe installs

    • Bitkey currently sells hardware in select countries; check the store for the latest list.
    • Apps are available in the Apple App Store and Google Play where regional compliance allows.
    • Emergency Exit Kit instructions assume access to Google Drive or iCloud and an Android device.

    Quick answers

    Can Bitkey see my balances or transactions?

    No. Keys stay on your devices and Bitkey cannot sign without you; the app can connect to your own node for extra privacy.

    Who gets notified if someone starts a recovery?

    You receive push, SMS, and email alerts, and any Recovery Contacts you designated see the request in their app.

    Does Bitkey keep my biometric data?

    Fingerprints stay on the hardware key; Bitkey only tracks whether biometrics are enabled.

    Bitkey is a mobile and hardware wallet developed by Block and designed for people new to Bitcoin who want simple, secure self-custody. The system combines a phone app with a wireless hardware device and an encrypted cloud backup, using a 2-of-3 multisig model to keep users in control of their funds. This structure allows users to recover access even if they lose their phone or the device, adding safety without relying on seed phrases.

    The hardware, a sleek hexagonal device with a fingerprint sensor and NFC for phone pairing, eliminates the need for complex seed phrases, making it user-friendly. Sending Bitcoin requires both your phone and the Bitkey device, while smaller transactions can be approved using just your phone and Bitkey's server key if you've set spending limits. This balance appeals to people who want more security than just a phone wallet but don't want the complexity of most hardware wallets.

    Users can set daily spending limits for app-only transactions, adding flexibility. The setup is quick, often taking just 5 minutes, and the app's clean interface is intuitive. The app allows Bitcoin purchases, shows balance in both sats and fiat, and offers recovery through trusted contacts in case of loss. It also lacks a screen, meaning you can't visually verify addresses on the hardware itself.

    Bitkey's mission is to bring secure Bitcoin ownership to a global audience, especially those new to Bitcoin, by removing friction and avoiding the pitfalls of traditional backups. Their future vision includes broader international support, expanded wallet functionality, and continued emphasis on open-source development and user control. For beginners, Bitkey offers a straightforward, secure way to own Bitcoin with a polished user experience, though advanced users may want more flexibility.

    FAQs

    How does Bitkey's 2-of-3 key system keep my bitcoin safe?

    Bitkey splits control between the mobile key on your phone, a fingerprint hardware key, and a server key, and every send or security change needs two signatures. That means Block can never move funds alone while you still have a path to recover coins if one device fails.

    What is Bitkey's Cloud Recovery flow if I lose my phone?

    The Bitkey app stores an encrypted copy of your mobile key inside your iCloud or Google Drive account, and the hardware key decrypts it. Install Bitkey on a new phone, sign into the same cloud account, tap the hardware key, and your wallet restores without seed phrases.

    What happens when my Bitkey hardware is lost or damaged?

    Start hardware recovery in the app and Bitkey triggers a seven-day security waiting period while SMS and email alerts confirm the request. After the hold, pair a replacement device or invite Recovery Contacts to approve sooner so you can co-sign again.

    How do Recovery Contacts help if I lose my phone and hardware?

    You can pre-approve trusted contacts in the Bitkey app, and they install Bitkey, accept your invite, and agree to vouch for you. If both keys disappear, you share a code with them, they verify it inside their app, and that lets you rebuild your wallet on new hardware.

    When would I use the Bitkey Emergency Exit Kit?

    The Emergency Exit Kit is a signed Android utility plus an encrypted mobile key backup stored in your cloud drive. If Bitkey's infrastructure ever goes offline, you sideload the kit, tap your hardware key, and sweep your bitcoin to any address you control.
