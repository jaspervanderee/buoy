    Hodl Hodl is a peer-to-peer (p2p) Bitcoin trading and lending platform based in London that stands out by being fully non-custodial and privacy-focused. Instead of holding funds, it uses multisig escrow contracts, so users retain control of their Bitcoin until both parties agree to release it, shifting trust from the exchange to individual traders, as trades happen directly between user's Bitcoin wallets rather than a centralized entity.

    What separates Hodl Hodl from custodial platforms is its ability to let people trade without KYC (Know Your Customer) or central control, supporting over 100 currencies and various payment methods including cash, bank transfers, even PayPal. You do pay a higher premium on Bitcoin set by the seller, especially when you're a beginner and have to build a reputation on the platform.

    The web-based interface is beginner-friendly, praised by users for easy signup without mandatory ID checks, though users note clunky navigation and scam risks from unresponsive traders. To unlock significant transaction amounts, users must build trust through reviews and completed trades, as higher limits depend on a strong reputation score.

    Hodl Hodl's vision is to foster a decentralized Bitcoin economy, ideal for beginners who want self-custody and anonymity, but users should be careful, stay informed, and follow best practices during trades.

The vision is to offer a private, on‑the‑go way to trade Bitcoin without heavy identity requirements, making it attractive to beginners who want control and privacy without overcomplicating the process. Beginners love its privacy and ease, but some find the mobile-only platform limiting.

    FAQs

    Which supports more payment methods in my country—HodlHodl or Peach?

    Both list many local rails. HodlHodl commonly supports bank transfer, SEPA, Wise, Revolut and even cash-in-person. Peach offers bank transfer, SEPA, Wise, Revolut, Twint and cash-in-person.

    Do HodlHodl or Peach use escrow for disputes?

    Yes. Both are non-custodial P2P platforms that use multisig escrow for trades. Funds are locked in escrow until payment is confirmed.

    Do either require ID for higher limits?

    Peach is KYC-free for smaller trades but notes that limits and verification can apply based on region and payment method. HodlHodl is non-custodial and KYC-free by default.
