    Aqua Wallet, originally launched by Blockstream, later taken over by Jan3, is a mobile app designed for easy saving and spending in Bitcoin. It's a non-custodial, open-source wallet using a 12-word seed phrase for user control, and a unified balance across on-chain Bitcoin, Lightning, and Liquid Network transactions, defaulting to Liquid for low-fee, private payments. It offers sending, receiving, and swapping Bitcoin (on-chain to Liquid or vice versa) but for Lightning payments they rely on Boltz, a third party which can occur higher swap fees.     FAQs

    Is Aqua a Lightning wallet?

    Aqua is a non-custodial wallet focused on Bitcoin and Liquid. Lightning payments are supported via non-custodial Boltz swaps rather than native channels. That enables Liquid features and privacy benefits, with Lightning executed through swap transactions instead of on-device channel management.

    Is Breez custodial?

    No. Breez is a self-custodial Lightning wallet. It automates channel management behind the scenes and uses a cloud backup to restore wallet and Lightning state. Some users appreciate the convenience; funds remain in your control with seed-based recovery and automated infrastructure support.

    Which has lower fees for small payments?

    For pure Lightning, Breez’s native LN avoids swap steps. Aqua’s Lightning sends use Boltz swaps, which can add swap and on-chain components at times. Actual costs vary with network conditions and usage. Compare features, custody model, and recovery approach in the table.
