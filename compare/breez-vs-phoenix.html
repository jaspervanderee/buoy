It stands out with its non-custodial mobile app, letting users control their Bitcoin, and a unique Point-of-Sale mode for merchants to accept instant payments. It offers sending, receiving, and storing Bitcoin via Lightning, plus a podcast player to support creators with Bitcoin tips.

    The app has a simple interface and no-setup payments, though some users report issues with cloud backups and wallet restoration.

    Breez's mission is to drive Bitcoin adoption for everyday use, with a vision to expand Lightning payments across apps using its Breez SDK, a free developer tool. The app currently suits everyday payments rather than very small transactions. Beginners love its ease and merchant tools, but some find technical issues frustrating.

    Phoenix runs a native Lightning node on the user's device, connecting to ACINQ's node for automated channel management, eliminating the need for swaps and reducing fees for direct Lightning payments. Additionally, Phoenix allows connections to a user's own Bitcoin node for enhanced privacy.

    For beginners, Phoenix's intuitive UI, low-cost Lightning transactions, and self-custodial design make it ideal for those starting with Bitcoin, though channel size limitations may require occasional fee adjustments for frequent receivers. Compared to Breez's merchant-centric features or Aqua's Liquid-focused versatility, Phoenix excels in delivering a straightforward, cost-effective Lightning experience with robust privacy options.

    FAQs

    Which has lower fees to start?

    Both use native Lightning. Costs can include channel creation or resizing events (on-chain) and the Lightning route itself. Phoenix emphasizes a streamlined node on your phone; Breez adds cloud backup conveniences. Actual totals vary with usage patterns, liquidity events, and broader network conditions.

    Which is better for merchants?

    Breez includes a Point-of-Sale mode and creator tools like podcast streaming, making it attractive for merchant and tipping scenarios. Phoenix centers on a streamlined, on-device node for everyday Lightning payments. Choose based on POS needs versus a focused personal payments experience.

    Which is faster or more reliable?

    Both aim for quick Lightning payments. Phoenix runs a node on your phone for direct LN; Breez also uses native LN with automated management. Reliability depends on your channels, liquidity, routes, and connectivity. Either can perform well when properly funded and connected.
