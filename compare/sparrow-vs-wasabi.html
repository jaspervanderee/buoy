    Sparrow Wallet is a desktop Bitcoin wallet designed to give users full control over their bitcoin. It was created by developer Craig Raw and is open-source, privacy-focused, and ideal for both beginners and advanced users. The wallet works on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

    What separates Sparrow from other wallets is its balance between simplicity and advanced features. Beginners can start with a basic hot wallet setup and gradually explore more complex features like hardware wallet integration, multisig vaults, and UTXO control—all within the same interface. It gives users direct visibility into how Bitcoin works, showing transaction details, input and output flows, and allowing for precise control over fees and coin selection. This level of transparency helps users understand and manage their funds more deliberately.

    User reviews highlight how Sparrow scales with you as you learn. It is refered to as the most powerful and versatile Bitcoin wallet, and users praise its clean layout, customizable setup, and educational value. Despite its many features, the app's interface is considered clear and organized once users get past the initial learning curve.

    Sparrow's vision is to offer users complete sovereignty over their bitcoin. It avoids mobile compromises and instead focuses on a desktop experience that can integrate with cold storage, your own node, and advanced privacy tools. For beginners who want to grow into more advanced use cases without switching wallets, Sparrow offers a flexible and educational path to full control.

    Wasabi Wallet is a privacy-focused, open-source, non‑custodial Bitcoin desktop wallet launched by zkSNACKs. It routes all traffic through Tor and uses compact blockchain filters, keeping your IP and balance private. Its standout feature is built-in CoinJoin, a process that mixes your Bitcoin with others' to make it harder for anyone to trace your transactions, enhancing privacy without relying on third parties.

    The user interface is straightforward but functional, with a dashboard showing your balance, privacy progress, and transaction history, though it may feel technical for complete beginners. Setup involves writing down a 12-word seed phrase and an optional passphrase, which adds security but requires careful storage.

    What sets Wasabi apart is its strong focus on privacy through CoinJoin, which is seamlessly integrated and doesn't require manual setup, making it accessible for newcomers. It also allows advanced users to connect to custom coordinators via platforms like Nostr, ensuring privacy even if default services are unavailable.

    Wasabi is for people who want to own their Bitcoin without having to trust big companies. It hides your transactions by mixing them with others, runs silently in the background over Tor, and gives you full control—all on your own computer. Its goal is to make privacy easy and powerful, while building toward a future where you don't rely on any single server.

    FAQs

    Which wallet has native CoinJoin?

    Wasabi includes built-in CoinJoin for enhanced privacy.

    Do these wallets work with hardware devices?

    Both support hardware wallets. Sparrow emphasizes deep hardware wallet and multisig workflows; Wasabi supports hardware wallets as well.

    Can either wallet connect to my own node?

    Yes. Sparrow connects to Bitcoin Core or your own Electrum server (including over Tor). Wasabi can connect via supported setups with hardware wallets.
