    Peach Bitcoin is a mobile-only peer-to-peer platform for buying and selling Bitcoin. It stands out with its non-custodial model, using multi-signature escrow to secure trades without holding user funds, shifting trust to individual traders, and offering KYC-free (Know Your Customer) transactions up to 1,000 CHF daily, emphasizing privacy.

    Users must build trust through reviews and completed trades to unlock higher transaction limits. It supports over 100 payment methods, including cash and gift cards, with a 2% fee for buyers and free selling.

    Peach's mission is to make peer-to-peer Bitcoin trading the standard, promoting financial freedom, with plans to enhance Lightning Network support and expand globally, especially in Latin America and Africa. The vision is to offer a private, on‑the‑go way to trade Bitcoin without heavy identity requirements, making it attractive to beginners who want control and privacy without overcomplicating the process. Beginners love its privacy and ease, but some find the mobile-only platform limiting.

    RoboSats is a privacy‑first, Lightning‑native peer‑to‑peer exchange for buying and selling bitcoin with fiat. It is open‑source, requires no registration or KYC, and runs primarily over Tor (with I2P and an Android app available). Each trade uses a one‑time “robot” identity tied to a secret token you control, and all peer chat is end‑to‑end encrypted with PGP.     RoboSats is a privacy‑first, Lightning‑native peer‑to‑peer exchange for buying and selling bitcoin with fiat. It is open‑source, requires no registration or KYC, and runs primarily over Tor (with I2P and an Android app available). Each trade uses a one‑time “robot” identity tied to a secret token you control, and all peer chat is end‑to‑end encrypted with PGP. The flow is simple: make or take an order, both sides lock small Lightning bonds, the seller locks a Lightning hold‑invoice escrow, fiat moves, then sats are released.

    What separates RoboSats is how deeply privacy and safety are built into the mechanics rather than bolted on. One‑trade identities reduce linkability across orders, PGP‑sealed chat prevents metadata leakage, and Lightning hold invoices act as both escrow and fidelity bonds to discourage cancellations and fraud. Pricing is transparent and market‑relative, fees are low at 0.2% split between maker and taker, and optional on‑chain payouts are handled via swaps. Clear best‑practice guides help users pick lower‑risk fiat rails and avoid chargeback traps.

    The ideal audience is anyone who wants fast, low‑fee, no‑KYC bitcoin for modest amounts and is comfortable using Tor and a compatible Lightning wallet. Trade sizes are tuned to what routes reliably on Lightning today, which suits frequent smaller buys and sells. The UI is straightforward once you learn the token/robot model, and the documentation walks through wallet compatibility, order timing, disputes, and payment‑method hygiene to keep trades quick and clean.

    RoboSats’ vision is to keep P2P bitcoin simple, private, and resilient, with a path toward a federated network of coordinators sharing a unified order book to reduce single‑operator risk while preserving the familiar UX.

    FAQs

    Do I need Tor?

    RoboSats is Tor-first (with I2P and an Android option). Peach is a mobile app and doesn’t require Tor.

    Are trades Lightning-only on RoboSats?

    RoboSats is Lightning-native with hold-invoice escrow and small fidelity bonds. Peach uses on-chain multisig escrow.

    Does Peach require KYC?

    Not for smaller trades. For higher amounts, additional verification can apply depending on region and payment method.
